Conclusion


In this experiment, we explored the fundamental concepts of an Intrusion Detection System (IDS) and its critical role in identifying and responding to malicious activities within a network. By simulating network attacks and analyzing traffic patterns, we gained hands-on experience in monitoring, detecting, and responding to potential intrusions.

Through practical implementation, we were able to:

  • Understand the architecture and working of Host-based and Network-based IDS.
  • Deploy and configure open-source IDS tools such as Snort or Suricata for real-time detection.
  • Analyze network traffic logs to identify patterns associated with known attack signatures.
  • Create and test custom rules to detect specific intrusion attempts like port scanning or DoS attacks.
  • Learn how IDS alerts help system administrators respond promptly to security threats.

This experiment highlighted the importance of real-time traffic analysis and rule-based detection in maintaining network security. We also recognized the limitations of IDS, such as false positives and the inability to prevent attacks, emphasizing the need for integration with other security systems like firewalls and SIEM.

Additionally, we observed how regular tuning of IDS rules and continuous monitoring are vital for effective threat detection. The importance of keeping signatures updated and combining IDS with threat intelligence feeds was also evident for advanced protection.
