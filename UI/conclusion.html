Conclusion

In this experiment, we successfully explored how Nmap can be used for network scanning to gather critical information about active hosts, open ports, and running services.

Through practical application, we were able to:

  • Identify live hosts on a network using different scanning techniques.
  • Detect open ports and analyze the services running on them.
  • Understand the significance of different scan types such as SYN Scan (-sS), TCP Connect Scan (-sT), UDP Scan (-sU), and OS Detection (-O).
  • Observe how the Nmap Scripting Engine (NSE) enhances scanning capabilities.
  • Analyze the role of firewalls and intrusion detection systems (IDS).

This experiment emphasized the importance of network security and proactive monitoring. Open ports can serve as potential entry points for attackers, making regular scanning essential.

Furthermore, we explored how different scan parameters and timing options impact the efficiency of network scans. These concepts are crucial for ethical hacking, penetration testing, and cybersecurity defense.

Overall, this experiment reinforced the significance of Nmap in cybersecurity and network management, enabling professionals to maintain a secure digital environment.
